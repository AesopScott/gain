Accessibility Statement

Last reviewed: 2026-04-19 · Target: WCAG 2.1 Level AA · Platform: governainow.com

Our commitment

GAIN is an AI governance platform. We believe the people who use it — compliance leads, risk officers, privacy teams, auditors, external counsel — should be able to do their job regardless of how they interact with the web. Our commitment is to design, build, and maintain the platform so that people using assistive technology have substantially the same experience as people who don't.

We target WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance for all customer-facing pages of the authenticated application and the marketing site at governainow.com.

Scope

This statement covers:

  • The authenticated GAIN application at governainow.com — Dashboard, Team, Framework, Intake, Inventory, Risks, AIAs, Checklists, Policies, Settings, Pricing, Support, Help, and Admin pages
  • Marketing and legal pages served from the same domain (Pricing, Terms, Privacy, DPA, Sub-processors)
  • Transactional email templates sent from the platform

Third-party services embedded in the platform (Stripe Checkout, the Firebase Authentication UI, and Anthropic's API) are governed by their own accessibility statements and are not within the direct scope of this document.

Current conformance

As of the date above, we have verified or implemented the following:

  • Skip-to-content link. A visible-on-focus skip link appears as the first focusable element on every authenticated page and jumps past the navigation to the main content.
  • Landmarks. Each page declares <main>, <nav aria-label="Primary">, and <footer> landmarks for screen-reader navigation.
  • Keyboard operability. All interactive controls (links, buttons, form fields, tab triggers, modal dialogs) are reachable and operable with a keyboard. Focus is never trapped outside a modal.
  • Focus visibility. A consistent 2-pixel primary-color outline appears on all interactive elements on :focus-visible (keyboard focus), matching WCAG 2.4.7.
  • Dialog focus management. Modal dialogs announce themselves via aria-labelledby, trap Tab and Shift+Tab focus inside the dialog, close on Escape, and return focus to the element that opened them.
  • Live region announcements. Success and error toasts are emitted through an aria-live="polite" region so that screen readers announce them without interrupting the user.
  • Form labels. Every form field has a programmatically associated <label> or an aria-label. Password fields declare the correct autocomplete token.
  • Reduced motion. The prefers-reduced-motion: reduce media query disables non-essential animations and transitions for users who have opted out at the operating-system level.
  • Responsive design. The platform reflows to a single-column layout at ≤720 px viewports without loss of content or functionality, meeting WCAG 1.4.10.
  • Color contrast. Body text and interactive elements in the default Classic Light palette meet or exceed a 4.5:1 contrast ratio against their background.
  • Theme support. Seven palettes and a light/dark mode toggle are available; color is never the sole indicator of state (status pills and risk badges include text labels in addition to color).

Known issues

In the interest of transparency, the following items are known gaps or are only partially conformant today. We track them as work and plan to close each one in a future release.

  • Non-default palettes. Contrast in the Slate Dark, Ocean Dark, Forest Dark, Royal Dark, and Graphite Dark palettes has not been exhaustively audited against WCAG 1.4.3. The High Contrast and Classic Light palettes are our recommended baselines for assistive-technology users.
  • Wide data tables. The inventory, risk register, AIA, audit log, and policy tables scroll horizontally inside their card on narrow viewports rather than restructuring into a stacked card layout. This meets WCAG 1.4.10 reflow but is not an ideal experience; a stacked mobile card view is planned.
  • Modal focus order. The Tab focus trap in modal dialogs does not currently skip hidden form fields that may be inserted dynamically by feature-gated code paths. In practice this only manifests on the AIA and intake editor modals.
  • Keyboard shortcuts. We have not yet published a shortcuts reference. The only non-Tab keyboard shortcut today is Escape to close a dialog.
  • Video / audio. The platform does not currently publish video content; when we do, captions and transcripts will be supplied.

Assistive technology we test against

We verify changes to the authenticated application against:

  • NVDA on Windows with Chrome
  • VoiceOver on macOS with Safari
  • VoiceOver on iOS with Safari
  • Keyboard-only operation across the full application
  • 200% browser zoom
  • Windows High Contrast mode

Screen-reader testing is not exhaustive on every release — we prioritize it on changes that touch authentication, modal dialogs, forms, and navigation.

Report an accessibility issue

If you encounter any accessibility barrier while using GAIN, we want to hear about it. We treat accessibility reports as first-class bug reports and respond within two business days.

Please include, where possible:

  • The URL of the page where the issue occurred
  • Your browser, operating system, and (if relevant) assistive technology and version
  • What you were trying to do and what happened instead

Send reports to accessibility@governainow.com or file a ticket via the in-app Support page with the subject "Accessibility."

How we maintain this

  • Accessibility is reviewed as part of every pull request that touches the UI. We do not ship a regression to WCAG 2.1 AA conformance on the Classic Light palette.
  • This statement is reviewed at least annually and updated whenever the "Known issues" list materially changes.
  • Major releases are accompanied by a manual keyboard and screen-reader smoke test covering sign-in, dashboard load, creating a risk, creating a policy, opening a modal, and submitting a form.
  • We welcome independent accessibility audits from customers with formal procurement requirements; contact accessibility@governainow.com to arrange one.
