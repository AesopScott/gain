Overview

This DPA forms part of the agreement between Customer and GAIN for use of the Service (the "Agreement," typically comprised of our Terms of Service and applicable order form). It applies when GAIN processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer that is subject to the GDPR, UK GDPR, Swiss FADP, the CCPA/CPRA, or other data protection laws requiring a processor agreement.

In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Agreement regarding the processing of Personal Data, this DPA controls.

1. Definitions

  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by GAIN on behalf of Customer under the Agreement.
  • "Controller," "Processor," "Data Subject," "Processing," and "Personal Data Breach" have the meanings given in the GDPR.
  • "Sub-processor" means a third party engaged by GAIN to process Personal Data on Customer's behalf.
  • "Data Protection Laws" means all laws and regulations applicable to the processing of Personal Data, including the GDPR, UK GDPR, Swiss FADP, CCPA/CPRA, and applicable U.S. state privacy laws.
  • Other capitalized terms have the meaning in the Agreement.

2. Roles and Processing

For purposes of the GDPR and similar laws: Customer is the Controller of Personal Data uploaded or transmitted to the Service; GAIN is the Processor, processing Personal Data on Customer's documented instructions.

For purposes of the CCPA/CPRA: GAIN is a Service Provider. GAIN does not sell or share Personal Data and does not retain, use, or disclose it outside the direct business relationship with Customer or the permitted purposes of the Agreement.

The subject matter, nature, purpose, and duration of processing, the categories of Personal Data, and the categories of Data Subjects are described in Annex 1.

3. Customer Instructions

GAIN will process Personal Data only on Customer's documented instructions, which include the Agreement, this DPA, and Customer's use of the Service's documented features. GAIN will inform Customer if, in GAIN's opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws.

4. Confidentiality

GAIN ensures that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations (whether contractual or statutory) and are trained on their privacy and security responsibilities.

5. Security

GAIN implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. Current measures are described in Annex 2.

6. Sub-processors

Customer authorizes GAIN to engage Sub-processors. GAIN:

  • Maintains a current list of Sub-processors at governainow.com/sub-processors.
  • Imposes on each Sub-processor data-protection obligations substantially similar to those in this DPA.
  • Remains liable for each Sub-processor's acts and omissions regarding Personal Data.
  • Provides at least 30 days' notice of the addition or replacement of a Sub-processor (via the Sub-processors page, with email notice to Customer contacts who subscribe to updates).
  • Allows Customer to object on reasonable grounds during that notice period. If an objection cannot be reasonably resolved, Customer may terminate the affected Service.

7. Data Subject Rights

GAIN provides Customer with the tools and documentation needed to respond to Data Subject requests under Data Protection Laws (access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, objection).

If a Data Subject contacts GAIN directly, GAIN will promptly forward the request to Customer and will not respond substantively except to confirm that the request has been received and referred.

8. Personal Data Breach

GAIN will notify Customer without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours, after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer's Personal Data. The notification will include, to the extent known:

  • A description of the nature of the Breach, including categories and approximate numbers of Data Subjects and Personal Data records.
  • The likely consequences of the Breach.
  • The measures GAIN has taken or proposes to take to address the Breach and mitigate its effects.
  • A contact point for further information.

GAIN will reasonably assist Customer in meeting Customer's own notification obligations to regulators and Data Subjects.

9. Cross-Border Transfers

GAIN processes Personal Data primarily in the United States. Where Personal Data originates from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, transfers rely on appropriate safeguards, including:

  • The EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Commission Decision 2021/914), Module 2 or 3 as applicable, which are incorporated by reference and will be deemed executed upon Customer's acceptance of this DPA.
  • The UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU SCCs.
  • The Swiss FADP addendum to the EU SCCs.
  • The EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and UK/Swiss extensions, where certified.

Annex details required by the SCCs are specified in Annex 1 and Annex 2.

10. Audit

GAIN makes available to Customer, on reasonable request, information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, including third-party audit reports (e.g., SOC 2 when available, ISO 27001, and Sub-processor certifications).

Where a regulator or applicable law requires on-site audit, Customer and GAIN will agree in advance on scope, timing, notice, confidentiality, and cost. Customer will bear its own costs and will not disrupt GAIN's operations or other customers.

11. Return and Deletion

On termination or expiration of the Agreement, GAIN will, at Customer's election, return or delete Personal Data from active systems within 30 days of request, except where retention is required by law. Encrypted backups containing Personal Data may persist until the end of the standard backup rotation (up to 90 days) and remain subject to this DPA until deletion.

12. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set out in the Agreement. Nothing in this DPA affects the liability of either party to a Data Subject under Article 82 GDPR.

13. Term and Termination

This DPA takes effect when Customer accepts it (typically by signing the Agreement or clicking-through during signup) and continues until the Agreement terminates and all Personal Data has been returned or deleted in accordance with Section 11.

14. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the law specified in the Agreement. Where the EU SCCs apply, the law and forum selections in the SCCs control for the portions they cover.

Annex 1 — Processing Details

A. Parties

Data Exporter (Controller): Customer, as identified in the Agreement.

Data Importer (Processor): GAIN, operating governainow.com. Contact: privacy@governainow.com.

B. Categories of Data Subjects

  • Customer's employees, contractors, and authorized users of the Service.
  • Individuals identified in Customer's governance records (e.g., named risk owners, policy approvers, system owners, incident reporters).
  • Individuals identified in consent records or disclosure logs that Customer uploads.

C. Categories of Personal Data

  • Contact data: name, email address, job title.
  • Account data: authentication credentials (hashed), role, workspace associations.
  • Usage data: IP address, user-agent, activity timestamps, audit log entries.
  • Content uploaded by Customer: any personal data included in inventory entries, risks, AIAs, policies, incident reports, contracts, disclosures, consent records, or free-text fields. Customer is responsible for ensuring PHI, payment card data, and other sensitive categories are not submitted through the AI policy-generation feature.

D. Special Categories of Personal Data

The Service is not designed to process special-category data. Customer must not upload PHI, SSNs, payment card data, or other sensitive personal data to the AI policy generation feature. Where Customer chooses to upload documents containing special-category data to structured fields (e.g., consent records), Customer remains responsible for the lawful basis of doing so.

E. Nature and Purpose of Processing

Hosting, storing, organizing, displaying, and transmitting Personal Data as necessary to provide the Service, including AI policy drafting (which involves transmission of Customer-provided prompt text to Anthropic's API).

F. Duration

For the term of the Agreement and any retention period described in the Privacy Policy and this DPA.

G. Frequency of Transfer

Continuous, during Customer's use of the Service.

Annex 2 — Technical and Organizational Measures

Access Controls

  • Role-based access within each Customer workspace (owner, admin, editor, viewer).
  • Firestore security rules enforcing per-workspace isolation at the database layer.
  • Least-privilege access for GAIN personnel; production access limited to personnel with a defined operational need.
  • Multi-factor authentication required for GAIN personnel accessing production systems.

Encryption

  • In transit: TLS 1.2 or higher for all Service traffic.
  • At rest: AES-256 encryption for Firestore, Cloud Storage, and backups via Google Cloud default encryption.

Authentication

  • Customer authentication via Firebase Authentication, which hashes passwords using industry-standard algorithms.
  • Session tokens expire and rotate automatically.
  • Account lockout and anomalous-login detection provided by Firebase Authentication.

Logging and Monitoring

  • Application-level audit logging of material actions within a workspace (who, what, when), visible to workspace admins.
  • Infrastructure logging via Google Cloud Logging.
  • Alerting on function errors, quota exhaustion, and anomalous authentication events.

Incident Response

  • Documented incident response procedure including triage, containment, eradication, and post-incident review.
  • Breach notification within 72 hours of confirmation (see Section 8).

Backups and Recovery

  • Scheduled Firestore exports to Google Cloud Storage.
  • Backups encrypted at rest; rotation on a defined schedule (up to 90 days).
  • Recovery procedures tested periodically.

Vendor Management

  • Sub-processors selected based on a security and privacy review.
  • Data-protection obligations flow down to all Sub-processors.

Personnel

  • Confidentiality obligations for all personnel handling Personal Data.
  • Privacy and security training on hire and annually.
  • Access revoked promptly on role change or termination.

Physical Security

  • Personal Data is stored in Google Cloud data centers, which maintain physical security controls described at cloud.google.com/security.
